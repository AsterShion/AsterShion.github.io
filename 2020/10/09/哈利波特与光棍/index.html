Why don’t you like Valentine’s Day?

I think Valentine’s Day is probably the worst holiday on the books. It’s engineered to make people feel bad. Not sure how it is in this day and age, but when I was a kid, there was no convention that demanded a kid must give Valentines to the entire class. So the popular kids would get tons, and the unpopular kids (like me) wouldn’t get very many.

I remember fifth grade, when my mom was urging me to take a bag to school “to bring home all the Valentines you’re going to get” and feeling a pain in my stomach. Ha, carrying them home was no problem. I only got three. One from the teacher – a Xeroxed piece of pink paper with a hand-drawn heart and my name spelled wrong.

As I got older, I did get Valentines, but again, most of them were cringeworthy. I remember one boyfriend spending $75 on some crappy roses and me thinking ‘why didn’t you just buy me a watch or something?’

Restaurant and hospitality workers, I’m told, hate Valentine’s Day. Everything is prix-fixe (and overpriced) to make up for the fact that it’s all two-tops, and that a lot of people don’t go out very often and tend to be bad guests. Undertipping seems to be a problem.

The imagery associated with Valentine’s Day tends to be ugly and cheeseball. I’m not a big fan of Christmas either, but I can appreciate the music, the lighting, the winter scenes and the like.

And it’s not even like they give you the day off! Actually, I do like seeing all the flowers come in to the office Valentine’s Day, that’s pretty cool. Even though I’d rather not get flowers on that particular day due to price-gouging, it’s fun to see others get them. If I was still an unpopular outcast, I might think differently, though.

Does anyone like it, I wonder? I know it’s one of the top days to get engaged and give jewelry, when I worked with De Beers there was always a big push from Xmas up until Valentine’s Day. But shit, I wouldn’t want to get engaged on Valentine’s day, just as I wouldn’t want to get married at Disney World.

The fact that it’s a Hallmark holiday doesn’t bother me. Everything is a marketing event, it seems, it’s just that this one was conceived and popularized by the B-team – the A-team was busy working on Christmas.

  我想情人节可能是书中最糟糕的的节日了。它的设计让人感到很糟糕。我不是很确定在今天这个时代情人节是怎么样的，不过当我还是一个孩子的时候，没有要求小孩子必须给全班情人节礼物这样的惯例。因此受欢迎的孩子会得到很多礼物，而那些不受欢迎的（就像我）不会得到那么多。

  我记得五年级的时候，当妈妈要求我背着书包去学校的时候说“把你所有得到的情人节礼物带回家”，当时我蛋都疼了。哈，把它们都带回家没有问题。我只得到了三个。其中一个是老师送的——一张粉色的纸条，上边有一个手绘的爱心，以及我被写错的名字。

  当我长大后，我收到了情人节礼物，但是类似的状况，大多数都让人很尴尬。有一回男朋友话了75块钱买了一些稀烂的玫瑰花，我在想“为什么你不送我一块表或者别的什么？”

  餐馆里那些好客的工作人员告诉我，他们讨厌情人节。一切都是这里的客饭（价格过高），以掩盖一个事实很多人并不常常出门，以及他们是糟糕的客人。小费给得可怜也是个问题。

  和情人节有关的印象变得不堪蹩脚。我也不热衷于圣诞节，不过我能在其中享受音乐，灯光，冬景诸如此类。

  这简直不像是在放假，事实上我很喜欢在情人节看到鲜花。那非常漂亮。虽然我不太希望在特定的节日那天得到价格奇高的鲜花，不过看到其他人收到了还是很有意思的。假如我还是一个不受欢迎的人，我可能会有不一样的看法。

  所有人都喜欢它么，我很好奇？我知道的，这一天很多人都去订婚并送出珠宝首饰，我还在De Beers 上班的时候从圣诞节那天到情人节总是有很多的业务。不过，我并不想在情人节那天订婚，就像我不愿意在迪士尼乐园结婚一样。

  事实上，这不过是一个普通的节日，并没有对我造成困扰。一切不过是营销活动。这不过是一个被一些人设计并使其流行开来的事件罢了——和那些在圣诞节忙活那波人差不多。
